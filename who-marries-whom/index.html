By John Zuwasti Curran
June 20, 2016

When it comes to falling in love, it’s not just fate that brings people together — sometimes it’s their jobs. We scanned data from the National Bureau of Statistics’ 2014 Census of the Maldives — which covers all 68,249 households — to find out how people are pairing up. Some of the matches seemed practical (the most common marriage is between grade-school teachers), and others had us questioning Cupid’s aim (why do female dancers have a thing for male welders?). High-earning women (doctors, lawyers) tend to pair up with their economic equals, while middle- and lower-income women often marry up. In other words, female CEOs tend to marry other CEOs; male CEOs are OK marrying their secretaries.
